Board games started off being a tool for learning and entertainment for the wealthy. Used to teach aspects of war or practice anticipating moves of opponents.

As the middle class came into prominence on the world stage, their capital and free time lead to the acceleration of the development of games.

This volume of different titles is coupled with the diversification of genres and game mechanics, which lends itself to a rich tapestry of ideas and ludemes.

Lastly I leave you the reader an ability to play, as "Play is older than culture, for culture, however inadequately defined, always presupposes human society, and animals have not waited for man to teach them their playing." - Huizinga, Homo Ludens
